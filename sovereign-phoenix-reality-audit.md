## 3. The five things I oversold (in priority order)

### 3a. The 2.5 → 4 stars in 18 months timeline is highly aspirational

Real-world data on NY MA star ratings:

- **The 2025 industry average is 3.92 stars, *down* from 4.04 in 2024**. CMS removed extreme outliers from the methodology, making 4+ stars genuinely harder to reach for everyone.
- **4+ star status correlates with plans that have 10+ years of program experience.** SP is a distressed 2.5-star IPA, not an established plan.
- **SAIPA — your own reference — took ~5 years from inception in 2020 to reach 4.6 stars.** SAIPA also runs a focused 120-PCP cohort within one community. SP is 400 mixed providers across multiple boroughs and demographics — *harder* to align.
- **Industry consensus: "several years of sustained effort" is the realistic timeline** for 2.5 → 4 stars. Not 18 months. **3–4 years** is the honest number.

**Implication:** The bull case 4-star achievement at Y3 is plausible. The base case at Y2 mid is a stretch. The dashboard's "4 stars by Y2" framing was too aggressive — push the Aetna $12 PMPM unlock to Y3 in any realistic model.

### 3b. The Health First "25K incoming lives by mid-Y2" assumption is unsubstantiated

I modeled this as a near-certain event in Base and Bull. In reality:

- HF HEDIS cleanups for distressed 2.5-star IPAs **typically take 12–24 months** to clear from baseline, not 6 months.
- HF approval of bulk member transfer is contractually complex and gated quarter-by-quarter, **not a single event**. The "25K landing" framing is wrong — it lands in tranches or gets delayed.
- Even after HEDIS cleanup, **HF retains discretion on member assignment** to networks. "We will give you 25K" requires written contractual commitment, not handshake.
- The 70/30 Medicaid/Medicare split assumption is reasonable but **unverified**. Could easily be 80/20 — and Medicaid SS yield is materially thinner than Medicare.

**Implication:** Base case should assume **partial cleanup landing in Y2 (~10–15K lives)** with the remainder in Y3 or never. The "25K by mid-Y2" projection in the dashboard is the most aggressive single assumption in the entire model.

### 3c. The Aetna 750 → 6,000 lives growth path requires 8× expansion in 3 years

Real MA panel growth for an IPA is typically **15–30% per year organically**. Achieving 8× in 36 months requires:

- Major Aetna cooperation on attribution rules
- Significant new PCP onboarding (not just star-uplift on existing panel)
- Lincoln-data targeting to actually convert prescribers to attributed panels — and provider conversion is **slow** (6–12 months per provider to fully attribute their Aetna MA patients)

**A 2–3× growth (750 → 2,000) is plausible. 8× to 6,000 is the upper edge of believable.** Bull case is genuinely a stretch goal.

### 3d. Provider distribution was missing entirely from the original model

This is the one you flagged, and it's the biggest single math correction:

- **NY VBP regulation requires a minimum of 40% of shared savings to be distributed to the provider contractor.** This is the regulatory floor, not a negotiation point. Below 40% likely fails quality scoring.
- **Typical NY IPA structure distributes 50%** of net shared savings to providers after operating expenses.
- My original model showed **100% of shared savings flowing to IPA partners**, which is structurally impossible under NY VBP rules and would gut provider engagement (the very thing DocPro needs to drive quality).
- **This single correction reduces base case per partner by ~50%** — from $1.15M to ~$240K at the 50% distribution scenario.

### 3e. Operating costs are 30–50% higher than I modeled

I modeled OpEx at $900K–$1.5M ramping with lives. Realistic for a 30K-life IPA running a SAIPA-tier quality program:

- Care managers / quality nurses: $600–900K (multiple FTEs needed)
- DocPro platform allocation: $200–400K
- Quality analysts + data infrastructure: $200–400K  
- Compliance, legal, contracting: $150–300K
- Provider engagement (Aaron + Ahmed time-allocated): essentially free as partner equity contribution
- **Realistic total: $1.2M–2.0M annual at scale**, not $900K–$1.3M

Partner sweat equity (Aaron, Ahmed, Luis, DocPro working without salary in early years) is the main reason this isn't worse, but **assume $1.2M Y1 / $1.5M Y2 / $1.8M Y3** as the honest run-rate.
