Whether it be not completing tasks to the end, or purposely making the wrong decision, each and every event seems to compound upon one another. Its almost as if one step in the wrong direction opens the "floodgates" to a series of unfortunate actions.

My mind continues to create excuses or outing for every seemingly difficult task at hand whether "oh maybe its best we study now instead of hitting the gym this morning", to "eh ill do it later or ill start tomorrow". When the mind stays in a state of perpetual cognitive ease without structure, routine and disciple, it enables time slots for such behaviors. 

If your weeks are planned thoroughly without missing gaps and enough efforts is directed towards the task, there is no "dead zones" for weak decisions since scheduling simply doesn't allow such actions. The lack there of motivation in the morning is virtually non existent even when adequate sleep measures have been fulfilled.  Its the dead zones(time wasting) between morning activities, self sabotaging early fast breaks, and our body's inability to direct itself toward strenuous and painful activities. 

The re-introduction of bad habits that had once been defeated some time ago, that have re-integrated themselves seamlessly into my life without invitation. How much can one balance his learning, projects, hobbies , into one integrated schedule and still remain strict in many aspects. Whether it be pushing the last few reps till failure, or being able to say no to oneself before embarking on a series of bad decisions.
