# HEDIS gap reports — turn the lab into a recurring revenue lever

Standard lab pitch is reactive: "send us your specimens, we run them."
The version that actually wins clinics is **proactive**: monthly HEDIS
gap reports identifying patients who have fallen out of clinical
metrics, with clinical justification for follow-up appointments.

**Concrete examples surfaced at AFFA:**
- Diabetic kidney screenings (microalbumin, eGFR cadence)
- Abnormal lipid panels with no follow-up scheduled
- Pre-diabetic monitoring (A1C trend watching)
- GLP-1 medication candidates by BMI + comorbidity profile (weight management program funnel)

**Why this matters:**
This reframes the lab as a *practice growth tool*. Every monthly gap
report = a list of billable follow-up visits the practice would
otherwise lose. The lab gets the volume from those visits; the practice
gets the revenue. Aligned incentives.

**Sales motion:**
Lead with the gap-report capability when pitching practices with stable
patient panels (vs. acute-volume practices). Quantify: "200-patient
diabetic panel × 30% missed quarterly screening = 60 reschedulable
visits/month at $150 office charge = $9k/month in recovered revenue
the practice didn't know it was leaving on the table."

**Open question:** can the gap-report engine run from the eClinical
integration alone, or does it need separate EMR-data feed setup?
Worth confirming before pitching this as a primary lead.
