# Specialty Pharmacy Sales-Pitch Framework

Captured 2026-05-14 after the Parmar + Langan GI intros both landed the same morning using this exact framing.

## The pitch (one sentence)

> "Doc, our goal is to get those expensive biologics your patients sit on for the lowest out-of-pocket cost possible and delivered in a timely manner."

## Rules

1. **Keep it short.** The less information given to the physician, the simpler the close.
2. **Give them the answer they're looking for** — don't info-overload.
3. **The real sellers are the PA/office staff**, not the doctor. The doctor is the green-light; the office is the throughput.
4. **Win the office staff and keep them happy.** They submit orders direct to Insight Outpatient Pharmacy.
5. **Pitch the PA value loop:** better PA quality at the office → lower expense burden on our PA team → we can do this at scale.

## Why this works

- ~40% PA acceptance rate on expensive biologics is the office staff's daily pain. Solving that for them = trust.
- Doctors don't want education — they want a competent partner who handles the cost-and-delivery problem.
- Long, complete pitches give physicians more reasons to say "let me think about it." Short pitches force a binary: yes-or-no on a single value prop.

## When NOT to use this framing

- When the doctor IS the PA-quality decision-maker (small solo practices where the doctor does their own PAs)
- When the office uses a 3rd-party PA service and our value-add needs to differentiate against that
- When the doctor asks for technical/clinical specifics — switch to detail mode but stay terse

## Operational follow-up after pitch

1. Ilene receives CDTM form from office
2. Office submits orders direct to Insight Outpatient Pharmacy
3. We monitor first-month order flow + PA acceptance
4. 30-day check-in with office manager (NOT the doctor) on satisfaction
