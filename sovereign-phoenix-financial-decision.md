## 4. The revenue engine — Aetna Medicare math

### 4a. Admin-fee step function (per attributed Medicare Advantage life)

| Star rating | Aetna PMPM admin | Annualized per life |
|---|---|---|
| 2.5 (today) *(assumption)* | ~$4 – $5 | $48 – $60 |
| 3.0 | ~$6 – $7 | $72 – $84 |
| 3.5 | ~$8 – $9 | $96 – $108 |
| **4.0 (target)** | **$12** *(stated)* | **$144** |
| 4.5+ *(stretch)* | $14 – $16 *(assumption)* | $168 – $192 |

**Implication.** Every full star is worth roughly $3–4 PMPM in admin fee, or
~$36–48 per attributed life per year, independent of shared savings. **The
2.5 → 4.0 uplift is itself the biggest single dollar lever in the deal.**

### 4b. Aetna shared savings — Level 2, 50% upside / 50% downside (Y1 no-downside)

- **Level 2 = 50% upside AND 50% downside** for the contract — i.e., if actual
  medical cost beats benchmark, the IPA keeps 50% of the savings; if it runs
  over benchmark, the IPA owes back 50% of the overrun.
- **Y1 carve-out: NO downside in the current contract year.** This is the
  honeymoon that fixes the year-one cash gap. **From Y2 onward downside is
  live** — this is a real, contractually defined obligation, not theoretical.
- **Payouts in ~3 months** — quarterly reconciliation, paid in the following
  quarter. The fast cycle is what makes the structural fix work for Y1.
- **CRITICAL diligence item:** the Aetna contract should specify a **downside
  cap** (typically % of benchmark or fixed $). The cap is the actual floor on
  the bear case. **Pull this number before signing.**
- **NOT pursuing ACO REACH.** SP will not be applying as a REACH ACO. The
  Aetna MA contract is the engine; no Medicare FFS / direct-contracting
  revenue layer is in scope.
- Realistic shared-savings *capture* per attributed life (IPA share, post-50%
  split) *(assumption)*:

| Performance level | IPA share per life per year |
|---|---|
| Bear (limited cost mgmt, weak coding) | $50 – $150 |
| Base (DocPro fully active, mid-cycle) | $250 – $400 |
| Bull (SAIPA-tier execution) | $500 – $800 |

### 4d. Health First — the volume engine

- **$2 PMPM admin fee, Level 1 contract.** "Super-generous" per the deal

- **25% upside-only shared savings** (no downside) — Level 1 structure.
- **Up to 25,000 additional lives incoming** once HEDIS cleanup clears,

  Medicare). This is the single biggest revenue lever in the deal.

  - Medicare lives in HF: ~$200–300/life/yr at IPA share
  - Blended on 25K (70/30): ~$100/life/yr → **~$2.5M/yr at steady state**
- Pre-cleanup baseline (existing HF lives in SP): estimated ~5K *(assumption)*.

### 4e. Fidelis — the steady baseline

- **$3 PMPM admin fee, Level 1 contract.**
- **Shared savings on ~2-year lag** (legacy contract structure) — minimal Y1
  cash, ramps Y2+.
- Total Fidelis lives in SP: unknown, modeled at ~3K *(assumption — refine

## 6. The self-funded buyout mechanic (why this is a flywheel, not a single bet)

The $250K gets you 51%. The remaining **49% sits with existing ownership** and
can be bought out over time using the shared-savings cash the IPA throws off.

Illustrative buyout pace *(assumption — actual will hinge on what the existing
owners demand and the discount you negotiate as performance improves)*:

| Trigger | Action |
|---|---|
| Q3–Q4 Y1 first shared-savings payout lands | Begin negotiating second tranche (e.g., +10% for $X) using performance + a relatively low-leverage seller |
| 4-star achievement | Lock a pre-negotiated buyout schedule for remaining ~39%, paid quarterly from Aetna distributions |
| 18-month performance proven | Full buyout becomes financeable (bank/MSO debt, since now de-risked) |

The flywheel: **as quality goes up, the cash the IPA throws off goes up, your
leverage to buy more of it goes up, and your willingness to pay for it goes up
the same amount — but the seller's leverage goes DOWN once they see the
turnaround.** This is why the "$250K is the floor, not the ceiling" framing is
actually favorable to the buyers.

## 12. The honest caveat — what this model can and cannot tell you

This document maps the math conditionally on a chain of assumptions, all of
which need real-world confirmation before you wire anything:

- The **$12 PMPM at 4 stars** number is your figure; have Luis pull the actual
  Aetna contract language — specifically (a) the $12 PMPM at 4 stars, (b) the

  writing, and (d) the **Y2+ downside cap structure** (% of benchmark or fixed
  $). The downside cap is now the single most important diligence number —
  it is the actual floor on the bear case.
- **ACO REACH is NOT in scope.** SP will not be applying as a REACH ACO; no
  Medicare FFS / direct-contracting revenue layer is in the model. The
  Aetna MA contract is the engine.
- The **Aetna attributed-lives** are anchored on the 750 current + the user's
  sober tier ladder (1.5K / 3K / 6K). Practice context: ~400 providers ×
  ~250 patients/month ≈ ~100K touches/month. The Lincoln data lets you
  replace the bear/base/bull guess with a real Aetna MA attribution number,
  and that single substitution sharpens this whole document by an order of
  magnitude.
- **Health First cleanup is the largest single revenue dependency.** The
  ~25K incoming HF lives at 70/30 Medicaid/Medicare drive the largest single
  block in Base and Bull (HF SS alone is $2.5M+/yr at steady state). Verify
  with Luis (a) HF's actual HEDIS cleanup acceptance criteria, (b) target
  transfer date, (c) actual member-mix breakdown. If the cleanup doesn't
  land by mid-Y2, the deal collapses to bear regardless of Aetna stars.
- **Fidelis lives** are a placeholder (~3K) — confirm with the actual
  Fidelis contract to size that baseline revenue.
- The **shared-savings capture rates** ($50–$800 per life per year) are
  benchmark ranges, not your contract's specifics. Once DocPro shows you the
  SAIPA history, the base/bull bands tighten significantly.
- The **OpEx assumptions** treat DocPro's data platform as a partial-cost
  reuse from SAIPA. If DocPro insists on standing up a Sovereign-Phoenix-only
  instance, opex rises ~$300K/yr and break-even moves up by ~2K lives.
- The **MiroFish 68% / 89%** is a stakeholder-dynamics forecast, not a

**The decision this document supports is "stage commitment behind the gates,"
not "wire the money."** When Gate 1 (Health First HEDIS pre-validation) clears
