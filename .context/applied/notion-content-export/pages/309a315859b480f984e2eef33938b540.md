#### Partnership Structure
- Agreement to bring providers under Saipa IPA using DBA (Doing Business As) structure
- Tax ID remains Saipa's, but multiple DBAs can be created for different ethnic communities (e.g., United Eastern Europe IPA, Middle Eastern IPA)
- Saipa maintains legal entity ownership while DBAs handle operations
- Different "faces" for different communities to overcome cultural barriers and build trust
- Providers will be credentialed under Saipa's tax ID but marketed through community-specific DBAs

#### Revenue Sharing Discussion
- Health First contract structure: 75% to health plan, 25% to IPA
- Of the IPA's 25%, providers receive 50-75% based on performance, remainder split between Saipa and partners
- First year proposal: 10-15% to Aaron/Ahmed's team, with potential for renegotiation after year one
- Revenue comes from shared savings and quality incentives, not membership fees
- Shared savings paid out with significant delay (Fidelis pays 2 years in arrears, Health First pays by March for previous year)

#### Insurance Contract Details
- **Health First**: Most transparent, 50/50 shared savings, provides constant updates on care gaps
- **Fidelis**: 50/50 shared savings but complex calculations and long delays; reduced quality bonuses in 2025
- **Molina**: 85% Medicaid, 90% SSL, 90% Medicare, 70/30 shared savings
- **VNS**: 120% reimbursement rate, 50/50 shared savings
- Enhanced vs Essential rates: Once provider reaches 750+ members, rates double (e.g., $30 becomes $60)

#### Provider Recruitment Strategy
- Target 20,000-30,000 patients through direct provider recruitment
- Focus on Arab community providers and Eastern European providers not currently in IPAs
- Many targeted providers already have Health First contracts but are fee-for-service, not value-based
- Priority on Medicare patients as highest revenue source
- Aaron has access to Health First panel data through lab connections to identify high-volume providers

#### Timeline and Onboarding
- Critical to onboard providers by April/May to have 6-8 months for quality improvement before year-end
- Provider loading takes 1-1.5 months after credentialing submission
- Summer period ideal for working with providers before busy season in September
- Need to assess provider utilization and quality before accepting to protect Saipa's overall metrics

#### Quality and Utilization Management
- Quality incentives significant: Dr. Ryan example receiving $600,000+ annually from quality bonuses
- Utilization management critical - poor utilization by one provider can drag down entire IPA
- Saipa's team will handle all quality work initially while partners facilitate provider relationships
- Provider education needed on utilization since most only track quality metrics
- Risk stratification, HCC coding, and care gap closure require significant ongoing work

#### Risk Considerations
- Bringing in poorly performing providers could lower Saipa's overall quality scores and cost money
- Example: Score drop from 4.6 to 4.4 costs significant money; score increase of 0.7 points gained $161,000
- First year expected to have "hiccups" but Saipa leadership willing to absorb initial losses to establish partnership
- Pre-assessment of providers essential before onboarding to avoid bringing in high-risk panels

#### Health First vs Other Plans
- Health First described as most transparent and best to work with
- Fidelis criticized for complex calculations and lack of transparency
- Health First wants to ensure quality providers join to protect their 75% share
- MetroPlus offers no value-based contracts, only care coordination fees
- VNS presents growth opportunity with good contract terms

#### Background Context
- Mohamed previously worked for Fidelis as Provider Relationship Manager (2010-2020)
- Fidelis-WellCare merger happened unexpectedly in June 2020 despite CEO denials
- Saipa currently has strong relationship with Health First leadership including direct access to executives
- KIPA is only other MSO approved by Health First, with 200,000 members
