#### Hospital at Home Program Requirements
- ACO has value-based program with Medicare with capitated patients
- Building hospital at home program requiring urgent blood draws (within 1 hour) for acute status changes
- Lab confirmed they can accommodate stat draws and build out workflow
- Current volume estimate: 10-15 high-value hospital at home cases per week
- Discussed extended hours coverage (up to 6 PM, potentially later with surcharge) for after-hours draws

#### Service Capabilities and Turnaround Times
- Lab has 20-30 phlebotomists covering home draws in the area
- Standard turnaround: Results within 24 hours if patient seen same day
- Routine orders: Patient outreach within 24-48 hours, scheduled within a week
- Stat service available with same-day draws and results in a couple hours
- Coverage area extends from Ossining to Staten Island

#### Patient Population and Logistics
- Patients are homebound, mostly dual eligible Medicare/Medicaid
- Multilingual needs across all NYC boroughs: Spanish, Russian, Chinese, Bengali
- Patient availability window typically 9 AM to 2-3 PM
- Common challenges: patients in bed unable to answer door, not answering phones
- Lab will keep patients flagged in system for ongoing outreach until contact is made

#### Testing Capabilities
- All standard tests available: CBC, CMP, hemoglobin A1C, lipid panels
- Advanced UTI PCR testing: 24-hour results (vs 2-3 day culture), 99% accurate, includes antibiotic resistance genes
- HPV self-swab program for cervical cancer screening gap closure (95% accuracy, measures CIN2/CIN3 lesions)
- Stool PCR panel (GPP) with 19 targets including C. diff, results in 24 hours vs 7-10 days
- Traditional iFOBT cards for colorectal cancer screening
- In-house pathology services

#### Insurance and Billing
- Lab bills Medicare directly for home visit draws using homebound patient CPTs
- Covers major Medicare plans: HealthFirst, VillageCare, ElderPlan
- MetroPlus contract in negotiation, estimated 1-2 months to completion
- Current volume: Practice does 500-700 labs per week total

#### Current Lab Partners
- Currently working with BioReference (main partner) and LabCorp
- Previously worked with Northwell (Queens/Long Island), Quest (limited home draws)

#### Data and Technology Integration
- ECW interface integration required (lab pays $1,500-2,000 one-time fee)
- Bidirectional interface needed to receive orders and send results back to patient charts
- Lab provides patient portal access via email or phone number with linked results
- Dashboard portal showing care gaps, missed tests, diagnosis-based panels (A1Cs, lipids, iFOBTs)
- 15-minute automated critical value alerts can be set up directly to provider line

#### Critical Values Workflow
- Lab monitors criticals 24/7 with immediate alerts
- Customizable critical value thresholds per practice preferences
- Options for notification: email, phone to clinical line, or team chat channels
- Practice has rotating on-call providers and hospital demand team for daytime criticals
