**Mon, 20 Apr 26**
## Specialty Pharmacy Program Restructuring
- Restructuring current program from profit-focused to genuine drug management
	- Moving from "edgy" money-making approach to sincere patient care
	- Backend payment structure maintained while improving patient outcomes
- Program covers biologics for multiple specialties
	- GI: Skyrizi, Mirabove, Winfall (3rd line treatment after salamine, creams fail)
	- Dermatology: Same biologics as 2nd line treatment after topical steroids
	- Rheumatology: First line for underlying diseases
- Patient prescription value: $1,007–$15,000 per treatment
## Target Market Analysis
- NYC doctor network potential
	- Every contact has ~3 doctors
	- 1 in 3 doctors typically enrolls in program
- Key specialty groups identified:
	- Gastro Associates of Brooklyn (3 locations: Midwood, Bay Ridge)
	- 10+ doctors, larger than current partnerships
- Patient demographics heavily Latino/Black offices for dermatology
- Disease prevalence low but memorable — doctors know their biologic patients
## Financial Structure & Reimbursement
- Buy-and-bill model for GI groups
	- Groups purchase drugs directly ($20,000+ per treatment)
	- Infuse patients in-office, bill insurance
	- Challenge: ensuring insurance covers at contracted rates
- Co-pay reduction program
	- Current patient co-pays: $1,800–$4,000
	- Program reduces to $80–$200
	- Major competitive advantage over standard care
- Prior authorization handling
	- Pharmacy manages all PAs instead of doctor's office
	- Removes administrative burden from practices
## Program Implementation Process
- Doctor enrollment requirements:
	1. Sign affiliation forms
	2. Provide patient demographics
	3. EMR integration setup
- Patient workflow:
	1. Prescription sent to specialty pharmacy
	2. Prior authorization processed
	3. Telehealth consultation for side effects education
	4. Medication shipped or infusion scheduled
- Language barrier consideration for Elmhurst location (large Spanish-speaking population)
## Strategic Approach & Next Steps
- Shift targeting strategy from doctors to practice managers
	- Need patient data before doctor meetings
	- Present concrete numbers: "150 patients on X drug, 75 on Y drug"
	- Demonstrate 80% co-pay reduction impact
- Time allocation: 50% specialty pharmacy, 50% other business
- Immediate focus: identify directly reachable targets without intermediaries
- Follow-up meeting scheduled at coffee shop
