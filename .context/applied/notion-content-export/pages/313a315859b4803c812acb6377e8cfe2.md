**Lab Panel Management Discussion** (2026-02-26)
<summary>
### Action Items
- [ ] Push for creation of order sets on the doctor's side
- [ ] Product and technical teams to evaluate options for managing compendium updates and custom panels
- [ ] Investigate syncing compendium updates to order sets and panel sets to prevent conflicts
- [ ] Extract client list by EHR (specifically Practice Fusion users)
### Key Issues Identified
- **Custom Panel Confusion**: Clients experience frustration when ordering tests because panel definitions vary between labs, leading to mismatches between what was ordered and what was expected
- **Compendium Update Challenges**: When the compendium is updated globally (approximately every six months), it affects all custom panels, potentially causing conflicts with doctor-specific preferences
- **Doctor-Specific Requirements**: Different doctors within the same clinic want different test combinations for similarly-named panels (e.g., "women wellness panel" vs "female wellness panel"), but each doctor only wants one test code
- **Naming Conflicts**: Panels with similar names create confusion between doctors, even when clinic names are added to panel names
### Proposed Solutions
- **Custom Order Templates**: Implementation of doctor-side order sets to allow doctors to define their preferred test combinations
- **Synchronized Updates**: Establishing a regular sync schedule (every six months) when compendium updates occur, with each clinic able to manage their own adjustments
- **Clinic-Level Customization**: Current system allows each clinic to create their own custom panels
- **Doctor-Level Panels**: Exploring the possibility of creating custom panels for individual doctors that aren't visible to all providers
### Technical Capabilities
- **EHR Integration**: Confirmed ability to extract client lists by EHR system, with Practice Fusion being particularly straightforward
- **Portal Limitations**: A self-service portal for making compendium changes may not be feasible
</summary>

**Revenue Opportunities Through MDLand EMR** (2026-03-04)
<summary>
### Revenue Enhancement Programs
- Medical practices are losing significant revenue opportunities due to underutilization of existing patient panels
- **PCMH (Patient-Centered Medical Home) Certification**: Generates $12-13 per patient per month (PMPM) in the New York territory without requiring additional patient visits
- With a 2,000-patient panel, practices can earn an additional $20,000 per month just from PMPM payments
- **Value-Based Care (VBC) Programs**: Practices can receive bonus payments of hundreds of thousands of dollars annually (examples cited of $500,000 year-end checks from Health First) by closing care gaps and achieving star ratings
- **CCM (Chronic Care Management) and RPM (Remote Patient Monitoring)**: Additional revenue stream of $110-130 per patient per month for enrolling patients with chronic conditions
- These programs are preferred by government payers because they reduce hospital admissions and overall healthcare costs
### Data-Driven Patient Engagement
- The key strategy is to maximize revenue from existing patient panels rather than constantly seeking new patients
- Lab integration enables practices to identify specific patient populations for targeted services (e.g., testosterone replacement therapy patients)
- Example workflow: Identify 150 patients with low testosterone, enabling the practice to convert them into regular visits (blood work every 3 weeks, follow-ups every 6 weeks)
- Data analytics help practices bring patients in 4 times per year instead of once annually, increasing both revenue and quality of care
- Care gap management for chronic conditions requires patients to return every three months, which aligns with value-based care requirements
### MDLand Platform Capabilities
- **Automation and AI**: Platform automates patient engagement through text messages and soon AI-powered phone calls to remind patients about appointments and needed follow-ups
- **Low no-show rates**: Automated reminders keep patients engaged and reduce missed appointments
- **Quick data integration**: Platform can complete lab data integration in as little as 16 minutes to 24 hours (record was 3:16 pm to integration complete)
- Standard data conversion and transfer takes 4-8 weeks, but practices can start using the system earlier with batch data processing
- **Electronic ordering**: Automatic e-order transmission to lab systems at end of day
- Platform provides dashboards and data analytics to help practices identify revenue opportunities from their existing 2,500+ patient charts
### Pricing and Setup
- **First provider**: $450/month (includes claims processing)
- **Second provider**: $350/month
- **Mid-levels**: $300/month
- Total cost for two providers: $700/month, which is 40% less than competitors
- Reduces staffing costs by eliminating need for additional front desk staff ($25-30/hour) through automation
### Competitive Advantages
- **vs. eClinicalWorks (ECW)**: ECW charges $600+ per provider ($1,200 for two providers); setup takes 3+ months with difficult integration requiring 3 hours just to build lab panel templates
- **vs. Practice Fusion**: Can provide data and reports even if practices don't switch to MDLand
- **vs. Medgen**: Described as problematic with slow issue resolution
- MDLand offers quick, simple setup with pre-built templates and panels ready to use immediately
- Described as the difference between modern smartphones and flip phones when compared to legacy systems
### Specialized Solutions
- **GI and surgical centers**: MDLand offers IGI product specifically for gastroenterology practices and surgical centers with imaging integration
- Team available to meet with surgical centers and urgent care facilities to discuss specific needs
### Referral Compensation
- $100 payment for introducing a practice to MDLand
- Additional $500 payment if the practice switches to MDLand
- Payment can be made directly to the referrer or included in their check
