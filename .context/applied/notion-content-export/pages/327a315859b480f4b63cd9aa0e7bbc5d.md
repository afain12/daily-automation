#### Partnership Structure and Revenue Model
- Partnership with Health First operates on 75-25 share savings split (Health First keeps 75%, IPA gets 25%)
- With Fidelis, the split is 50-50
- South Asian IPA currently has 44,000 members with Health First and maintains 5 out of 5 star quality rating
- Providers receive 150% Medicare rate plus $10 per claim and $5 per member per month
- IPA keeps 80% of share savings, partner organizations get 20%

#### Provider Recruitment and Onboarding Strategy
- Target providers currently with Bronx United IPA (capitation model with poor quality scores)
- Focus on convincing providers to switch from capitation to fee-for-service model
- Health First delegation allows new providers to be active following month if submitted by 14th
- Providers need 60-90 day notice to exit previous IPA contracts
- Key target neighborhoods: Bay Ridge, Ridgewood Queens, Flatbush Avenue Brooklyn
- Health First community contracts were frozen due to New York Presbyterian contract issues

#### Quality, Utilization, and Coding Education
- Three critical performance areas: quality metrics, utilization management, and proper coding
- Proper coding significantly impacts funding - can double reimbursement from $400 to $800 per patient
- Providers often under-code chronic conditions (amputation, CKD stages, diabetes complications, CHF, cancer treatment)
- Risk adjustment through proper documentation increases CMS funding to insurance companies
- IPA team will work directly with new providers on quality improvement and coding practices
- First-year providers may be negative but often turn around with education

#### Cost Management Priorities
- Hospital and emergency room visits are biggest cost drivers
- Pharmacy costs significant - providers need education on drug pricing
- Quest Lab identified as most expensive lab vendor, reducing share savings
- Daily data feeds from Health First and VNS track hospital admissions for intervention

#### Lincoln Lab Partnership
- Lincoln Lab positioned as preferred laboratory partner for IPA providers
- Quest's high rates negatively impact IPA revenue and share savings
- AccuReference identified as out-of-network for Health First - providers alerted
- Immigration patient lab work presents opportunity for competitive cash pricing ($200-300 range vs $500+)
- Lincoln Lab will have table at annual IPA party with promotional materials

- Network Solutions IPA (Joel Stern): partnership discussions did not proceed
- Health First prioritizes relationships with high-performing IPAs like South Asian IPA and Canadian American IPA

#### Provider Termination Process
- Providers wanting to switch IPAs must send a formal termination email with specific language stating the 90-day notice period
- The email must clearly state dates (e.g., "this is May 1st, May, June, July, July 30th I'm out") to establish the countdown
- Sample termination email templates are available for reference
- Helfer will support providers through the transition process, as their leadership prioritizes provider satisfaction

#### Helfer's Competitive Advantages
- Helfer has a provider-friendly approach and actively supports providers who want to switch IPAs
- Quality bonuses are sent directly to providers - no intermediary takes a percentage
- Providers can see their earnings directly through Helfer's quality app
- Helfer's philosophy is that providers remain with Helfer even if they change IPAs, so they want to keep providers satisfied
- Unlike FIDALIS, where providers have no recourse with problematic IPAs, Helfer takes action on provider complaints
